Section:Frequently asked questions
Q: What is A-Life Beyond Critical Care?
A: A-Life Beyond Critical Care is a non-participating plan which covers you until the age of 80. This plan provides comprehensive critical illness coverage for 180 conditions. They are broken down into 65 Early Stage Critical Illnesses, 40 Intermediate Stage Critical Illnesses and 75 Advanced Stage Critical Illnesses. Apart from that, this plan also provides Health Management Benefits, Yearly Cash Bonus, Personal Medical Case Management service, a Guaranteed Maturity Benefit, and a Compassionate Benefit.
Q: Who is eligible to buy A-Life Beyond Critical Care and A-Plus Beyond Advance Care?
A: A-Life Beyond Critical Care and A-Plus Beyond Advance Care are available to individuals aged between 16 to 60 years old.
Q: What is the coverage period for A-Life Beyond Critical Care and A-Plus Beyond Advance Care?
A: A-Life Beyond Critical Care and A-Plus Beyond Advance Care covers you until the age of 80.
Q: What is the minimum coverage amount for A-Life Beyond Critical Care and A-Plus Beyond Advance Care that I can purchase?
A: The minimum coverage amount for A-Life Beyond Critical Care and A-Plus Beyond Advance Care is RM20,000.
Q: Will my premium increase as my age increases?
A: The premium rates are level and based on your age when the plan is issued.
Q: Are the premiums for A-Life Beyond Critical Care and A-Plus Beyond Advance Care guaranteed?
A: The premiums for this plan are not guaranteed and the Company reserves the right to revise the premium rates by giving the policyholders 3 months written notice.
Q: What are the fees or charges that I have to pay?
A: There are no fees and charges other than the premium amount.
Q: Are the premiums paid for A-Life Beyond Critical Care eligible for income tax relief?
A: Yes, the premiums paid for this plan may qualify you for a personal tax relief, subject to the final decision of the Inland Revenue Board of Malaysia.
Q: What are the major exclusions for A-Life Beyond Critical Care and A-Plus Beyond Advance Care?
A: Exclusions for A-Life Beyond Critical Care
1. Any illness or surgery other than a diagnosis of or surgery for a defined Critical Illness Event;
2. The signs or symptoms of the Critical Illness Event defined under Advanced Stage is manifested prior to or:
a) within sixty (60) days for Cancer, Heart Attack, Coronary Artery By-Pass Surgery, Serious Coronary Artery Disease, and Angioplasty and Other Invasive Treatments for Coronary Artery Disease; or
b) within thirty (30) days for all other Critical Illness Events defined under Advanced Stage; from the Issue Date or Commencement Date of the policy, whichever is later;
3. The signs or symptoms of the Critical Illness Event defined under Early Stage or Intermediate Stage, is manifested prior to or within sixty (60) days from the Issue Date or Commencement Date of the policy, whichever is later;
4. The Critical Illness Event arises directly or indirectly from a Pre-Existing Condition as defined, which existed prior to the Issue Date or Commencement Date of the policy, whichever is later;
5. The Critical Illness Event in the presence of any Acquired Immune Deficiency Syndrome (AIDS) or Human Immunodeficiency Virus (HIV) infection. We reserve the right to require the Insured to undergo a blood test for HIV as a condition precedent to acceptance of any claim. The exception is when Full-blown AIDS, HIV Infection Due To Assault, HIV Infection Due To Blood Transfusion, HIV Infection Due To Organ Transplant, and Occupationally Acquired HIV Infection as defined in the policy. For the purpose of this plan,
a) the definition of AIDS shall be that used by the World Health Organization in 1987, or any subsequent revision by the World Health Organization of that definition; and
b) infection shall be deemed to have occurred where blood or other relevant test(s) indicate in Our opinion either the presence of any HIV or Antibodies to such a Virus;
6. Any Critical Illness Event which was diagnosed due, directly or indirectly, to a congenital defect or disease, which was manifested or was diagnosed before the Insured attains seventeen (17) years of age;
7. Any Critical Illness Event which was caused by a self-inflicted injury;
8. Any Critical Illness Event resulting directly from alcohol or drug abuse; or
9. The Insured did not survive for at least fifteen (15) days after the diagnosis of a Critical Illness Event.
Exclusions for A-Plus Beyond Advance Care
1. Any illness or surgery other than a defined Critical Illness or Covered Surgery;
2. The signs or symptoms of the Critical Illness or Covered Surgery that is manifested prior to or:
i) within sixty (60) days for Cancer, Heart Attack, Coronary Artery By-Pass Surgery, Serious Coronary Artery Disease, and Angioplasty and Other Invasive Treatments for Coronary Artery Disease; or
ii) within thirty (30) days for all other Critical Illnesses or Covered Surgeries; from the Issue Date or Commencement Date of the policy, whichever is later;
3. Any Pre-existing Conditions prior to the Issue Date or Commencement Date of the policy, whichever is later;
4. The Critical Illness or Covered Surgery in the presence of any Acquired Immune Deficiency Syndrome (AIDS) or Human Immunodeficiency Virus (HIV) infection. We reserve the right to require the Insured to undergo a blood test for HIV as a condition precedent to any acceptance of any claim. The exception is when Full-blown AIDS, HIV Infection Due To Blood Transfusion, and Occupationally Acquired HIV Infection. For the purpose of this plan:
i) The definition of AIDS shall be that used by the World Health Organization in 1987, or any subsequent revision by the World Health Organization of that definition.
ii) Infection shall be deemed to have occurred where blood or other relevant test(s) indicate in Our opinion either the presence of any HIV or Antibodies to such a Virus.
5. Any of the Critical Illnesses or Covered Surgeries which was due, directly or indirectly, to a congenital defect or disease which has manifested or was diagnosed before the Insured attains seventeen (17) years of age;
6. Any of the Critical Illnesses or Covered Surgeries which was caused by a self-inflicted injury;
7. Any of the Critical Illnesses or Covered Surgeries resulting directly from alcohol or drug abuse; or
8. The Insured did not survive for at least fifteen (15) days after the diagnosis of the Critical Illness or undergoing of the Covered Surgery.

A-Life Beyond Critical Care is an innovative critical illness protection plan that covers critical illnesses from early to advanced stages. These are a few reasons why A-Life Beyond Critical Care is unique:
•180 critical illness conditions covered. •Get a Guaranteed Maturity Benefit of up to 150% of total premiums paid (excluding any loading premiums) if the critical illness benefit has not been fully paid to celebrate a clean bill of health when your policy matures at the age of 80.
With that, A-Life Beyond Critical Care is a critical illness plan that goes beyond the usual protection and focuses on prevention and early detection for a Healthier, Longer, Better Life.

Section:5. How do I explain the 180 critical illnesses to my client?
The 180 critical illnesses can be categorised into 76 categories which affects different parts of the human body. Please refer to the appendix for an illustration of the critical illnesses mapped to the affected body parts. For your customer’s reference, the same illustration can also be found in page 4 of the A-Life Beyond Critical Care brochure.
In addition to the most common critical illnesses like cancer, heart attack, stroke, and serious coronary artery disease, we have now included a category namely “intensive care” which is a general condition that can be used to cater for various conditions, either due to illness or accident, that can befall someone. Under the “intensive care” category, as long as the Insured is admitted into an Intensive Care Unit (ICU) for at least 5 consecutive days with mechanical ventilation and survive for at least 15 days after the diagnosis, 100% of the basic sum assured will be payable. Conditions that can potentially be covered with this category are ICU admission due COVID-19, Sepsis, Dengue, and Pneumonia.
For the complete definition of the critical illnesses, please refer to policy contract.

Section:53. How does the reduced-paid-up work for A-Life Beyond Critical Care?
The Cash Value for the entire policy (A-Life Beyond Critical Care and A-Plus Beyond Advance Care (if attached)) will be used to determine the Reduced Paid-Up sum assured which is payable in the event of the Insured’s death or maturity/expiry of this policy. When this policy has been converted to a Reduced Paid-Up Insurance, all the benefits and riders under A-Life Beyond Critical Care shall cease and terminate.
